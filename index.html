Abstract

Freshwater ecosystems are facing a crisis with extinction rates of aquatic species exceeding those of their terrestrial counterparts by up to fivefold. This study examines the distribution and habitat requirements of winter stonefly species (Allocapnia spp.) across three major river systems in Northwest Arkansas‚Äîone of the fastest-growing regions in the United States. Through intensive field sampling at 22 sites during two winter seasons (2020-2022), I identified critical land-use thresholds that significantly impact stonefly species richness and assessed the conservation status of three Species of Greatest Conservation Need.
